Overview

This project investigates the impact of haze on object tracking performance for multiple object tracking (MOT) and multiple object tracking and segmentation (MOTS) tasks. UNICORN, a more sophisticated model, and PCAN, a baseline model, are used for evaluation. The experiments are conducted on the BDD100K dataset, which is a large-scale diverse driving video dataset with comprehensive annotations. Haze is added to the dataset with varying levels of intensity (beta values of 0.01, 0.02, and 0.06), and various dehazing techniques are applied to assess their impact on the models' performance.

Results:

  • MOT and MOTS tasks are negatively affected by the presence of haze in the dataset.
  • UNICORN, being a more powerful model, is less susceptible to haze than the baseline PCAN model.
  • The performance of both models deteriorates with increasing haze intensity (higher beta values).
  • Dehazing methods improve the performance of both models in MOT and MOTS tasks, with varying degrees of success.
